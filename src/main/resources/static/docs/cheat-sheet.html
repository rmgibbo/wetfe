Core Attributes

The four core attributes, which are scored on scales from 1 to 20.

Constitution

CON

Measure of your physical health, strength, endurance, and tolerance to bodily poisons.

Dexterity

DEX

Measure of your physical agility, reflexes, acrobatics, and finesse with weapons & tools.

Intelligence

INT

Measure of your mental agility, perception, insight, logic, and detailed memory.

Willpower

WIL

Measure of your mental health, mettle, and strength of character & convictions.


Chips

The units of your lifeforce and its manifest conditions.

Soul Chip

Ⓢ

Represents a unit of your overall lifeforce, which may be healthy, damaged, or fatigued.

Power Chip

Ⓟ

Represents a unit of temporarily empowered lifeforce. Each power chip prevents one soul chip from being damaged, and then pops.

Affliction Chip

Ⓐ

Represents an acute injury or impairment. Each affliction chip prevents one damaged soul chip from being healed, and then pops.

Trauma Chip

Ⓣ

Represents a serious, enduring malady. Each trauma chip prevents all fatigued soul chips from being recovered after a sound rest, and remains until explicitly removed.

Deques

The three deques of chips that represent your physical and mental state.

Health Deque

Ⓟ
Ⓢ

The soul chips in this deque represent healthy lifeforce. Above them, power chips may accumulate, up to your power limit. At the end of any turn in which any number of power chips popped, the remaining power chips pop as well.

Damage Deque

Ⓐ
Ⓢ

The soul chips in this deque represent damaged lifeforce. Above them, affliction chips may accumulate during encounters. After sufficient time has passed after the encounter, all chips in this deque automatically pop.

Fatigue Deque

Ⓣ
Ⓢ

The soul chips in this deque represent fatigued lifeforce. Above them, trauma chips may accumulate, with grave consequences.

Turn Options

On each turn, you may do one of the following.

ACT

You may perform a skill, or attempt a feat.

MOVE

You are hastened, and may move up to two zones away, provided there is a reasonable path to do so.

BLITZ

All of your offensive (INT | DEX) reaction rolls are advantaged until your next turn. However, you may not make any defensive (CON | WIL) reactions during this time.

TURTLE

All of your defensive (CON | WIL) reaction rolls are advantaged until your next turn. However, you may not make any offensive (INT | DEX) reactions during this time.

Ability Types

Effect Types

Skill

A practiced action. Governed by one of the four core attributes.

Anatomical

Effects that primarily interact with the physical structure of a target.

Feat

An action accomplished by winning a contest or succeeding at a challenge.

Psychological

Effects that primarily interact with the mental state of a target.

Reaction

An action that occurs by successfully responding to a trigger.

Infernal

Effects governed by the physics of the Infernal Realm.

Trait

A passive ability, which typically modifies another ability or the general rules for a character.

Celestial

Effects governed by the physics of the Celestial Realm.

Exotic

Effects governed by the physics of the Exotic Realm.

Confluent

Effects governed by the emergent physics born of the confluence of Realms.

Rolls

Skill

d20

Governed by one of your core attributes, a skill roll simulates you performing a practiced technique. The result ℜ is given by the associated skill table.

Feat

d20

Governed by one of your core attributes versus an opponent’s, a feat roll simulates a contest or a challenge. To succeed, you must roll better than the opposition’s roll. Feat rolls are thus always made in pairs.

Reaction

d20

Governed by one of your core attributes, a reaction roll simulates your success at reacting to a specific condition in combat, called a trigger. The result ℜ is given by the reaction table. (Typically, offensive reactions are governed by INT or DEX, while defensive reactions are governed by CON or WIL.)

Advantage

d20

Additional rolls that -- together with the original roll -- simulate an action, reaction, or defense. The final result ℜ is the best of all the rolls.

Disadvantage

d20

Additional rolls that -- together with the original roll -- simulate an action, reaction, or defense. The final result ℜ is the worst of all the rolls.

Enhancement

d6

A companion roll to a skill roll. The result, given by the enhancement table, directly modifies the result ℜ of the skill roll.

Enfeeblement

d6

A companion roll to a skill roll. The result, given by the enfeeblement table, directly modifies the result ℜ of the skill roll.
