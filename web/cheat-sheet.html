Core Attributes

The four core attributes, which are scored on a scale from 1 to 20.
(10 indicates the aptitude of a capable adventurer.)

Constitution

CON

Measure of your physical health, strength, endurance, and tolerance to bodily poisons.

Dexterity

DEX

Measure of your physical agility, reflexes, acrobatics, and finesse with weapons & tools.

Intelligence

INT

Measure of your mental agility, perception, insight, logic, and detailed memory.

Willpower

WIL

Measure of your mental health, mettle, and strength of character & convictions.


Chips

The units of your lifeforce and its manifest conditions.

Soul Chip

Ⓢ

Represents a unit of your overall lifeforce, which may be healthy, damaged, or fatigued.

Power Chip

Ⓟ

Represents a unit of temporarily empowered lifeforce. Each power chip prevents one soul chip from being damaged, and then pops.

Affliction Chip

Ⓐ

Represents an acute injury or impairment. Each affliction chip prevents one damaged soul chip from being healed, and then pops.

Trauma Chip

Ⓣ

Represents a serious, enduring malady. Each trauma chip prevents all fatigued soul chips from being recovered after a sound rest, and remains until explicitly removed.

Stacks

The three stacks of chips that track your physical and mental state.

Health Stack

Ⓟ
Ⓢ

The soul chips in this stack represent healthy lifeforce. Above them, power chips may accumulate, up to your power limit. At the end of any turn in which any number of power chips popped, the remaining power chips pop as well.

Damage Stack

Ⓐ
Ⓢ

The soul chips in this stack represent damaged lifeforce. Above them, affliction chips may accumulate during encounters. After sufficient time has passed after the encounter, all chips in this stack automatically pop.

Fatigue Stack

Ⓣ
Ⓢ

The soul chips in this stack represent fatigued lifeforce. Above them, trauma chips may accumulate, with grave consequences.

Turn Options

On each turn, you may do one of the following:

ACT

You may perform a technique, cast a spell, or attempt a feat.

MOVE

You are hastened, and may move up to two zones away, provided there is a reasonable path to do so.

BLITZ

You are hastened, and all of your offensive (INT | DEX) reaction rolls are advantaged until your next turn. However, you may not make any defensive (CON | WIL) reactions during this time.

TURTLE

You are hastened, and all of your defensive (CON | WIL) reaction rolls are advantaged until your next turn. However, you may not make any offensive (INT | DEX) reactions during this time.

Ability Types

Damage Types

Technique

A physical action. Governed by constitution or dexterity.

Anatomical

Effects that directly attack the bodily structure of a target.

Spell

A mental action. Governed by intelligence or willpower.

Psychological

Effects that directly attack the mental state of a target.

Feat

An action accomplished by winning a contest or succeeding at a challenge.

Infernal

Effects that embody the characteristics of the Infernal Plane.

Reaction

An effect that occurs by successfully responding to a trigger.

Celestial

Effects that embody the characteristics of the Celestial Plane.

Trait

A passive effect, which typically modifies an ability or the general rules for a character.

Exotic

Effects that embody the characteristics of the Exotic Plane.

Rolls

Action

d20

Governed by one of your core attributes, an action roll simulates you performing a technique, or casting a spell. The result ℜ is given by the associated action table.

Feat

d20

Governed by one of your core attributes versus an opponent’s, a feat roll simulates a contest or a challenge. To succeed, you must roll better than your opponent, or higher than the set difficulty of the challenge.

Reaction

d20

Governed by one of your core attributes, a reaction roll simulates your success at reacting to a specific condition in combat, called a trigger. The result ℜ is given by the reaction table. (Typically, offensive reactions are governed by INT or DEX, while defensive reactions are governed by CON or WIL.)

Advantage

d20

Additional rolls that -- together with the original roll -- simulate an action, reaction, or defense. The final result ℜ is the best of all rolls.

Disadvantage

d20

Additional rolls that -- together with the original roll -- simulate an action, reaction, or defense. The final result ℜ is the worst of all rolls.

Enhancement

d6

A companion roll to an action roll. The result, given by the enhancement table, directly modifies the result ℜ of the action roll.

Enfeeblement

d6

A companion roll to an action roll. The result, given by the enfeeblement table, directly modifies the result ℜ of the action roll.
